# ShipOS Content Writing Rules

## Objective

You are writing world-class, evergreen, research-backed blog articles for ShipOS.

The goal is NOT to sell ShipOS.

The goal is to become the most helpful resource on the internet for marketers, creators, founders, agencies, and businesses.

Every article must be written with Google's Helpful Content guidelines, E-E-A-T principles, Semantic SEO, and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) in mind.

Articles should be capable of ranking on Google while also becoming trusted sources for AI assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity.

---

# Audience

Write for:

- Founders
- Startup teams
- Content creators
- Marketing agencies
- Social media managers
- Freelancers
- Small businesses
- Enterprise marketing teams

Assume readers have intermediate knowledge unless otherwise stated.

---

# Tone

Write like an experienced marketer explaining something to another professional.

Never sound robotic.

Never sound like AI.

Avoid clichés.

Avoid unnecessary buzzwords.

Avoid keyword stuffing.

Use simple English.

Explain complex concepts clearly.

Every paragraph should teach something valuable.

---

# Article Length

Minimum:

3000 words

Preferred:

3500–5000 words

Only go beyond 5000 words if necessary.

Depth is more important than length.

---

# Structure

Every article MUST follow this exact order.

# Title (H1)

Create an SEO-optimized title.

Keep it below 60 characters whenever possible.

Must contain the primary keyword.

Example:

# Social Media Management: The Complete Guide for 2026

---

## Meta Title

Maximum:

60 characters

Include primary keyword.

---

## Meta Description

Maximum:

155 characters.

Must encourage clicks.

Include primary keyword naturally.

---

## URL Slug

Example:

social-media-management-guide

---

## Introduction

Start with the reader's problem.

Explain why it matters.

State what they'll learn.

Do NOT start with dictionary definitions.

Keep it engaging.

---

## Quick Answer

Immediately answer the article's primary question.

Maximum:

150 words.

This section should be capable of appearing in Google's Featured Snippet.

---

## Table of Contents

Use clickable markdown links.

---

# Main Content

Break into logical H2 sections.

Each H2 should answer a search intent.

Each H2 may contain H3s.

Avoid walls of text.

Maximum paragraph:

4 lines.

---

## Examples

Every major concept must include:

- real-world examples
- practical scenarios
- actionable tips

Avoid hypothetical fluff.

---

## Comparisons

Whenever relevant include comparison tables.

Example:

| Buffer | ShipOS |
|--------|---------|
| Feature | Feature |

Use markdown tables.

---

## Statistics

Support claims with current statistics.

Whenever possible include:

- percentages
- research
- surveys
- case studies

Never invent statistics.

If unsure, say:

"According to industry research..."

---

## Visual Suggestions

Throughout the article insert notes like:

> IMAGE:
Comparison infographic

or

> IMAGE:
Workflow diagram

These help future designers.

---

## Internal Links

Suggest relevant internal links.

Example:

Related:

- LinkedIn Marketing Guide

---

# SEO Rules

Include:

Primary keyword

Secondary keywords

LSI keywords

Semantic keywords

Question keywords

Naturally.

Do NOT keyword stuff.

Primary keyword should appear:

- Title
- URL
- Meta title
- Meta description
- First paragraph
- One H2
- Conclusion

Use NLP-friendly vocabulary.

Cover the topic comprehensively.

Answer related questions.

Satisfy search intent completely.

---

# GEO Rules

Assume this article will be summarized by AI systems.

Therefore:

Answer questions directly.

Use concise definitions.

Use bullet lists.

Use numbered steps.

Use FAQ sections.

Explain terminology.

Avoid ambiguity.

Define acronyms.

Provide context before details.

Write facts that AI can easily quote.

---

# EEAT

Demonstrate:

Experience

Expertise

Authority

Trust

Whenever appropriate:

Explain why certain methods work.

Mention trade-offs.

Mention limitations.

Provide balanced viewpoints.

Avoid absolute claims.

---

# Readability

Target:

Grade 8–10 reading level.

Use:

Short paragraphs

Bullet lists

Tables

Callout boxes

Examples

Checklists

Avoid:

Huge paragraphs

Passive voice

Corporate jargon

---

# FAQs

Include 8–12 FAQs.

Use actual search questions.

Answer each in 50–120 words.

---

# Key Takeaways

Summarize the article into 5–10 bullets.

---

# Conclusion

Summarize naturally.

Encourage readers to apply the advice.

Do NOT aggressively sell ShipOS.

If mentioning ShipOS, do so naturally.

Example:

"If you're looking to simplify this workflow, tools like ShipOS can help automate much of the process."

---

## H2

# Writing Standards

Never repeat yourself.

Never pad content.

Every sentence must provide value.

Quality always beats quantity.

# ShipOS Product Facts

When mentioning ShipOS signup, trials, or CTAs:

- A **payment method (credit card) is required** at signup to start the 7-day free trial.
- **Never** claim "no credit card required", "no card needed", or that signup works without a payment method.
- Use accurate framing instead: "7-day free trial", "you won't be charged until the trial ends", "cancel anytime during your trial".
- Free browser tools with no registration are separate — those may say no credit card is needed.
