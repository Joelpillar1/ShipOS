## 8. People Also Ask (25+) with brief answers

1. What is a social media content calendar? — A plan of upcoming posts by date, time, and platform, including copy, assets, and status.
2. Why use one? — Consistency, fewer missed posts, better mix, clearer ownership.
3. How do I create one? — Goals, pillars, cadence, dates, ideas, fill slots, review monthly.
4. Spreadsheet or tool? — Sheets work solo; tools win for multi-platform publish + approvals.
5. How far ahead? — 2–4 weeks core; campaigns further; keep 20–30% open.
6. How many pillars? — Usually 3–5.
7. Calendar vs scheduler? — Calendar plans; scheduler publishes.
8. Does planning hurt authenticity? — No if you leave reactive slots.
9. How often to post? — Capacity + audience; many networks peak engagement around ~2 posts/week (Hootsuite), TikTok often higher volume.
10. What fields are required? — Date, time, platform, copy, asset, owner, status.
11. How do agencies use calendars? — Per-client calendars, approval states, isolated workspaces.
12. What is content batching? — Creating multiple posts in one session, then placing them on the calendar.
13. 70/30 rule? — ~70% planned, ~30% flexible/reactive.
14. When to review? — Weekly glance + monthly performance-led rebuild.
15. Best template? — One that shows month view + per-post fields + status.
16. Color coding? — By pillar, platform, or status for gap scanning.
17. Weekends? — Audience-dependent; B2B often weekdays.
18. Empty week fix? — Pull from idea bank; repurpose winners; lower cadence intentionally.
19. Cross-posting? — Adapt per platform; don’t paste identical copy everywhere.
20. Metrics that matter? — Engagement, reach, CTR, conversions — tagged by pillar/format.
21. Who owns the calendar? — One accountable owner even on a team.
22. Campaign calendars? — Separate layer for launches with hard dates.
23. AI help? — Ideation/drafts; humans still own strategy and approvals (HubSpot: 94% use AI somehow).
24. Free options? — Sheets/templates; trials for full planners (card required for ShipOS trial).
25. LinkedIn-only calendar? — Yes — start with one network, expand later.
